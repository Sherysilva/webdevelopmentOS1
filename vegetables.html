1. Local Farms
Local farms are often a primary source of fresh vegetables for supermarkets. These farms are typically located within a few miles to a few hundred miles of the supermarket.

2. Wholesale Markets
Wholesale markets, also known as produce markets or food hubs, are central locations where farmers, distributors, and wholesalers sell large quantities of vegetables. Supermarkets often buy in bulk from these markets.

3. Importers and Distributors
Supermarkets often work with importers and distributors to source vegetables that are not locally available, either due to climate or seasonal constraints. These entities handle the logistics of importing produce from other states or countries
